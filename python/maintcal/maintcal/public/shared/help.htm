Maintenance Calendar User Documentation

 

 

Overview

The Maintenance Calendar application allows the user to perform the following functions regarding the scheduling of maintenance for redacted customers.

 

1)      Schedule a maintenance for customer

2)      View all currently scheduled tasks for a specific calendar

3)      Maintain the Service Types that can be scheduled for a customer

 

 

Features

The following features are now available in the Maintenance Calendar.

1)      Time zone awareness – no need to calculate the local time in order to schedule the maintenance.

2)      All required calendars are considered when determining available time slots to schedule.  No more looking at individual calendars to find a common available time slot.

3)      User ability to maintain Service Types (aka Tasks).  Change the times (length and lead) for existing Service Types.

4)      Know at a glance how much time is available for any given day.

5)      UI that will allow users to set the maximum number of bookings per time slot.

6)      Allows users to determine number of bookings as well as block time slots in the calendar

 

 

Affected Calendars

The first phase of the new Maintenance Calendar will affect the following calendars:

            DCOPs calendar for each of the DC’s

            Intensive Support Calendars (Windows & Linux)

            Managed Support Calendars (Windows & Linux)

            Managed NetSec

            Intensive Professional Services

            Managed Storage

 

All other current Maintenance Calendars will remain in the existing system.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Setting your local Timezone

The first time you access the Maintenance Calendar either through the Scheduling view, Calendar view, or Service Type Admin view, you will need to set your local time zone for the maintenance calendar.  You will do this by selecting a region and then a city within the region.  This setting will be persisted and remain in effect until your cookies are removed.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Scheduling Maintenance

 

From an existing support ticket, select the Schedule Maintenance option from the “Schdl Maint.” dropdown.  The “Scheduling Maintenance” screen will be displayed.

 

 

 

 

Expand the “Maintenance Category with Service Types” tab.  This will display the Maintenance Categories that are available to schedule. 

 

Expand the Maintenance Category to display the available Service Types that may be scheduled.

 

 

With the lists expanded, click on the Service Type that is to be scheduled.

 

It is very important to select the Service Type from the correct Maintenance Category.  The Maintenance Category determines which maintenance calendars are involved in the maintenance.  If an incorrect Maintenance Category is selected then the maintenance may be scheduled in the wrong calendars. 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Expand the “Selected Devices” tab to see the list of devices that the maintenance may be scheduled for.  The list is initially populated with the devices linked to the Master Ticket that the maintenance is being created from.  To add additional devices, click the gear icon on the “Selected Devices” to view a list of all devices for the account associated with the Master Ticket.  Click the check box to select the devices for the maintenance.

 

 

 

Once the Maintenance Category, Service Type and devices are selected, click the “Update Available Times” button.  The system will then determine what times are available to schedule the maintenance.

 

When determining the available time slots, the system takes the following into account:

            The Calendars that are involved

            The length of time for the Service Type

            The lead time required for the Service Type

 

If you select 4 or more devices for the maintenance, a warning will appear instructing you to contact the associated groups to determine if additional time will be required to complete the maintenance successfully.

 

 

 

View the available times by clicking on any day in the calendar.  The list of available times will be displayed.

 

 

Available times are only determined for the month the calendar is looking at.  In order to see available times in a different month, change the calendar to the desired month.  Then click the “Update Available Times” button and the system will re-calculate available times for the new month.  You can view available times for more than one day by moving the available times window around and open another day.

 

            Date Color Codes

            Green – Approximately 2/3 of the day are still available

            Yellow – Approximately 1/3 - 2/3 of the day is still available

            Red – Approximately 1/3 of the day is still available

            Black – Entire day has been booked and is not available

 

Hints:

If you need to schedule the maintenance in a future month, change the calendar to that month and click the “Update Available Times” button.

 

If a customer has requested a specific day to perform the maintenance, click on that day to see what time slots are available.

 

Double click on the desired time for the day, and the “Tentatively Scheduled Maintenance Options” screen will be displayed. 

 

 

 

 

Enter the overall goal of the maintenance in the General Maintenance Notes section.

 

Update the Point of Contact for the maintenance if it is different from the person that is scheduling the maintenance.  By default the POC will be the person scheduling the maintenance

 

The bottom half of the “Tentatively Scheduled Maintenance Options” will have a tab for each Maintenance Calendar that is involved in the scheduled maintenance.

 

For each tab, enter specific instructions for that calendar.  These instructions will appear in the sub-tickets that will be created for each queue involved in the maintenance.

 

Click the “Schedule” button on the “Tentatively Scheduled Maintenance Options” screen. The “Confirm Scheduled Maintenance” screen will be displayed.  At this point the time slot has been reserved so that no other person scheduling maintenance can use that time slot.

 

 

 

Click the “Confirm” button on the “Confirm Scheduled Maintenance” screen to schedule the maintenance.  The maintenance can now be seen when viewing the maintenance calendars.

 

Sub-tickets will be created for each of the Maintenance Calendars involved in the maintenance.  These tickets will be created New and Unassigned.

 

You will be returned to the window containing the Master Ticket that the maintenance was scheduled for.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Changing the Time Zone

You can change the time zone of the calendar when scheduling maintenance to the time zone of your customer.   You do not need to convert time when you are going to pick an available time.  It will be represented in the time zone that you have selected.

 

To set the time zone, click on the “Change Time Zone” button at the top of the “Scheduling Maintenance” screen.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

You will see a list of regions to choose from.  Select one of the regions and click OK.

 

 

Select the specific location within the region and click OK.  The calendar will now be represented in the selected time zone.

 

 

Click “Update Available Times” to see the available times reflected in the selected time zone.

 

 

Special Cases

 

To bypass the lead times for a Service Type, you can request the maintenance be Expedited.  To do this, expand the “Scheduling Options” tab and check the “Expedite” box.  At this point the system will re-calculate the available times by discarding the lead time.  You must obtain prior approval in order to expedite the maintenance.

 

 

When you check the “Expedite” box, warning will appear.  Click “OK” to clear the warning.

 

Click “Update Available Times” to see the available times reflected without the service type lead time taken in to account.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

There may be times when the standard length of time for a Service Type needs to be extended on a per maintenance basis.

 

To increase the required length of time for the Service Type beyond the standard length, select a time increment form the “Extend Length of Maintenance” drop down list.  The system will then re-calculate the available time slots based upon the new length of time for the Service Type (standard length + extended amount).  This will only apply to the specific maintenance that is currently being scheduled.

 

 

 

Click “Update Available Times” to see the available times reflected with the additional  time for the service type taken in to account.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Cancelling a Scheduled Maintenance

A previously scheduled maintenance may be cancelled by an Admin to the Calendars queue or by the user that scheduled the maintenance.  

 

From either the master ticket or sub-tickets for the maintenance go to the “Maintenance Info” box on the left side of the ticket.  You will then click there to see details of the maintenance. 

 

 

After clicking the hyperlink, the following screen will be displayed.  From this screen you can cancel the entire maintenance or cancel a portion of the maintenance.  This same screen will allow you to mark the maintenance as “Complete”.  In all cases either cancelling or completing the maintenance will cause the sub ticket status to be changed to Confirm Solved.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

You may cancel or complete a specific service by also going into the Maintenance Calendar view and view the details of the service.

 

 

 

On the calendar view, any maintenance that has been cancelled will be highlighted in Red, while a completed maintenance will be highlighted in Green.  You will see this when you select a day and view the maintenances scheduled for the day.

 

When a maintenance is cancelled that time slot is then made available and another maintenance can be scheduled in during that time.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Determining which calendars are involved

There are 3 main factors that determine which Maintenance Calendars are selected when the maintenance is scheduled.

 

            Maintenance Category

            Device Location

            Account segment and OS

 

DC Ops calendars will be selected if the Maintenance Category is Server Migration, Network Hardware Upgrade / Maintenance or Server Hardware Upgrade / Maintenance.  The specific DC will be selected based on the location of the devices involved in the scheduled maintenance.

 

Intensive Windows calendar will be selected if the account on the Master Ticket is in the Intensive segment and at least one server is Windows.

 

Intensive Linux calendar will be selected if the account on the Master Ticket is in the Intensive segment and at least one server is Linux.

 

Managed Windows calendar will be selected if the account on the Master Ticket is in the Managed segment and at least one server is Windows.

 

Managed Linux calendar will be selected if the account on the Master Ticket is in the Managed segment and at least one server is Linux.

 

Intensive Professional Services calendar will be selected if the Maintenance Category is Implementation Call, Server Migration, Network Hardware Upgrade / Maintenance or Network Software Upgrade / Maintenance / Walkthrough and if the account  on the Master Ticket is in the Intensive segment.

 

Managed Net Sec calendar will be selected if the Maintenance Category is Implementation Call, Server Migration, Network Hardware Upgrade / Maintenance or Network Software Upgrade / Maintenance / Walkthrough and if the account on the Master Ticket is in the Managed segment.

 

Managed Storage calendar will be selected if the Maintenance Category is Server Migration, Server Hardware Upgrade / Maintenance and any servers have an HBA SKU.

 

Managed Storage calendar will be selected if the Service Type is OS Install (Linux) or OS Install (Windows) and any servers have an HBA SKU.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Viewing Scheduled Maintenance 

 

Things you can do from this screen:

  • Select a specific Maintenance Calendar to view
  • Change the month for the calendar you are viewing
  • At a glance determine how much available time remains by day
  • View the currently scheduled maintenances for a specific day
  • Modify the number of bookings
  • Block and un-block specific time slots

 

 

To access the new Maintenance Calendar, select the “Maintenance Calendar” option from the CORE drop down menu list.

 

 

 

The Maintenance Calendars that are not initially moving to the new software will be accessed by selecting the “Legacy Maintenance Calendar” option.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Selecting a Calendar

Click the drop down menu and select the calendar you want to view.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Selecting a Month

Select the month you want to view using the forward and backward arrows to advance to the desired month. 

 

To jump to a specific month and year, click on the month name.  Select the desired month and year you want to jump to and click “OK”.

 

 

 

 

How much time is available?

The days are color coded to indicate the amount of time that is available to schedule maintenance for the day.

 

            Green – Approximately 2/3 of the day are still available

            Yellow – Approximately 1/3 - 2/3 of the day is still available

            Red – Approximately 1/3 of the day is still available

            Black – Entire day has been booked and is not available

 

View the scheduled maintenance’s

There are two ways to view scheduled maintenances for a specific day.

 

1.      Hover your mouse over a day for a quick view of what is currently scheduled.   The following information will be displayed:

                 Maximum bookings available for each time slot. 

                 Time slots that are already booked and brief description of the                                            maintenance

                 Time slots that are blocked

 

 

 

 

2.  Click on the day and the maintenances will be shown in the left side panel.   Click on

     a specific maintenance to see for details regarding the maintenance as well as get      

     links to the ticket, account or device.

 

 

Click box next to the specific maintenance to expand and see details of the maintenance.

 

 

All times displayed represent the local time for the user.

 

 

Modify Calendar Maximum Bookings

Click the gear icon on the “Add Block Time” tab.   The “Max Bookings” entry screen will pop up and display the current number of maximum bookings.  Modify the number and click OK.

 

 

The maximum bookings value is set for each specific maintenance calendar.  It is a global setting and applies to all days and all time slots with each day. 

 

If the maximum bookings needs to change within the day then you can do this by blocking time slots at the booking level (See “Block a Specific Time Slot” below).

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Block a Specific Time Slot

On the left side panel, click on “Add Block Time”.   Enter the From date, To date, and times that you want to have blocked.   This will block any maintenance from being scheduled at that time.  Time is blocked at the booking level.  When you block a time slot you will need to enter the number of bookings that the block applies to. 

 

 

For example:

The maximum booking for the calendar is set to 3.  However, third shift only has one team member available to perform maintenances.   To prevent over scheduling you can block the time frame for the third shift for two bookings.  This would still leave the one booking available for maintenance to be scheduled in.

 

To make the block re-occurring, you can check one of the “Repeating Options”.

       “Daily”: Use this to block a time range for each day in the date range. 

 

       “Weekly”: Use this to block a time range for a specific day of the week within the

                        date range.  The starting day in the date range determines the day of the

                        week to block. For example block 9-10 AM every Friday. 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Un-block a specific time slot

Select the time slot that you want to un-block and expand it.  Click either the “Remove” or “Remove Range” button.

 

 

“Remove” – unblocks just that specific time slot, all other blocks in the range will remain.

 

“Remove Range” – unblocks that time slot for the entire date range.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Service Type Administration

 

Things you can do from this screen:

  • Modify Service Types

                        Modify existing service types – Change service type length, change the

                        required lead time, remove service type

 

To access the “Service Type Administration” screen select the “Maintenance Calendar Admin” option from the CORE menu list.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

The “Service Type Administration” screen will be displayed.

 

 

 

To modify an existing Service Type, expand the Maintenance Category to see the list of Service Types.  Double click on the specific value that you want to modify and then enter the new value.  Both the length and lead time for a service type are entered in hours.

 

To remove a service type, uncheck the “Active” box for the specific Service Type.  The Service Type will still be listed but will not be able to be scheduled.  The maintenance scheduling process will only allow “Active” Service Types to be scheduled.

 

After making the desired changes, click the Save button to save your changes.  You must click Save in order for your changes to take effect.

 

Click the Cancel button to undo any changes without saving. 

 

 

Pre-scheduled maintenance

Any maintenance that is currently scheduled in the old calendar for a date equal to or greater than the new calendar rollout date will be imported into the new calendar system.  This will allow the users to view previously scheduled maintenance.  In addition this will allow the new system to take the previously scheduled maintenance into account when scheduling in the new system.
